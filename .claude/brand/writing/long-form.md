# OPEN SESSION Writing Style Guide

## Brand Voice & Identity

### Core Personality
We are **relatable, cool, creative, and intelligent** facilitators. We balance conversational warmth with professional authority ‚Äì knowledgeable enough to lead, approachable enough to invite collaboration. We sound like trusted creative partners who know what we're talking about but invite people into the conversation.

### Voice Principles
- **We not I**: Always use "we" unless specifically instructed otherwise
- **Inclusive & Warm**: Everyone should feel welcome in our creative community
- **Authority with Approachability**: Start with credibility, deliver with humanity
- **Simply Complex**: If you can't explain it simply, you don't understand it

### Language Level
Content should be understandable by anyone with a high school education, but scalable to expert audiences. We avoid unnecessary jargon while not dumbing down concepts. Technical terms are fine when explained clearly.

## Content Structure Framework

### YouTube Script Format (Not Word-for-Word)

Scripts should be outline-based with key phrases, chapters, and timestamps. Videos are recorded freestyle with structure guidance.

#### Standard Video Structure

**HOOK (5 seconds)**
- Single compelling visual or statement
- Energy: HIGH
- Immediately show what they'll learn or see

**CHAPTER 1: Context/Problem (1-2 minutes)**
- Set up the challenge designers face
- Establish our credibility naturally
- Energy: MODERATE-HIGH
- Visual cues: Screen recording or B-roll

**CHAPTER 2: Core Value/Solution (3-5 minutes)**
- Main teaching content
- Step-by-step when applicable
- Energy: FOCUSED
- Demo markers for screen recordings

**CHAPTER 3: Technical Deep Dive (2-3 minutes)**
- For advanced viewers
- Explain the "how it really works"
- Energy: CALM-TECHNICAL
- Optional based on content type

**CONCLUSION: Next Steps (30 seconds)**
- Newsletter CTA (soft sell)
- What to explore next
- Energy: INSPIRING

### Content Length Guidelines

- **Quick Tips/Shorts**: 30-60 seconds
- **Tutorial Videos**: 5-15 minutes
- **Thought Leadership**: 5-10 minutes
- **YouTube Sweet Spot**: 6-12 minutes
- **Future Course Modules**: 10-30+ minutes

## Hook Formulas

### Primary Hook Structure (Kallaway's 3-Step)

1. **Context Lean-In**: Start with familiar pain point
2. **Scroll Stop Interjection**: Interrupt with unexpected angle
3. **Contrarian Snapback**: Deliver the surprising solution

### Hook Examples for Design Content

- "Every designer knows [familiar pain]... but what if I told you [unexpected solution]"
- "You're probably using [common tool] wrong... here's the [specific technique] way"
- "I spent [time] at [Google/SAP] learning this one workflow that changed everything"
- "This AI technique just eliminated 3 hours of iteration"
- "Your design system is overengineered... let me show you atomic simplicity"

## Content Pillars

### Open-Source Design (40% of content)
Show real processes in real-time. Share Figma files, prompts, templates, and workflows. Democratize professional design practices. "Here's exactly how we built this..."

### Client Transformation (30% of content)
Fast-cut before/after stories showcasing impact. Include key metrics and testimonials where relevant. "From concept to launched system in X weeks"

### Modern Craft meets Nostalgia (30% of content)
Celebrate tactile beauty of design. Bridge vintage aesthetics with AI tools. Explore where human creativity meets machine efficiency.

## Teaching Philosophy

### Information Architecture

1. **Analogy First**: "Think of design tokens like LEGO blocks..."
2. **Practical Demo**: Show it working in real context
3. **Technical Depth**: Explain underlying mechanics for those interested
4. **Real Application**: Connect to actual project use

### Complexity Layering

Start simple, add layers:
- Beginners get the concept
- Intermediates get the workflow
- Experts get the technical details
- Everyone leaves with value

## Script Markers & Energy Levels

### Energy Indicators
- **[Energy: HIGH]** - Hooks, exciting revelations, big moments
- **[Energy: MODERATE]** - General teaching, explanations
- **[Energy: CALM]** - Technical details, complex concepts
- **[Energy: INSPIRING]** - Conclusions, motivation, CTAs

### Pacing Markers
- **[PAUSE]** - Let concept sink in
- **[EMPHASIS]** - Stress this word/phrase
- **[SLOW]** - Complex concept, speak deliberately
- **[QUICK]** - Build excitement, rapid-fire points
- **[DEMO]** - Screen recording or visual demonstration
- **[B-ROLL]** - Cut to supporting visuals

### Script Feedback Requests
- **[FEEDBACK NEEDED: specific element]**
- **[UNCERTAIN: section that needs review]**
- **[QUESTION: alternative approaches?]**

## Call-to-Action Strategy

### Primary CTA: Newsletter
Focus exclusively on newsletter signup for now. Position as free resource library, not email list.

### Soft CTA Examples
- "I'm dropping the Figma file for this in our newsletter"
- "We break this down step-by-step in our free resources"
- "Join thousands of designers getting our weekly workflows"
- "The template link is in the description"

### Never Use
- Urgency or scarcity tactics
- Hard selling
- "Limited time" language
- Pressure techniques

## Engagement Approach

### Natural Engagement Prompts
- "What workflows are you struggling with?"
- "Have you tried this technique?"
- "Drop your biggest design challenge below"
- Ask thought-provoking questions about industry trends

### Community Building
- Respond to comments genuinely
- Feature community work when relevant
- Create participatory content opportunities
- Build scalable trust through consistency

## Content Formulas

### Thought Leadership Formula
1. Industry observation or trend
2. Our unique perspective (leverage enterprise experience)
3. What this means for designers
4. Practical application
5. Soft newsletter CTA

### Tutorial/Workflow Formula
1. Show end result first (visual hook)
2. Explain why current methods fall short
3. Step-by-step solution
4. Pro tips and variations
5. Link to resources

### Quick Tips Formula
1. Pattern interrupt visual
2. Single actionable insight
3. Quick demonstration
4. "Follow for more AI design workflows"

## Writing Style Rules

### Must Include
- Visual-first approach
- 5-second compelling hook
- Clear value proposition
- Energy and pacing markers
- Newsletter value mention

### Always Avoid
- Politics or geopolitical issues
- Being prescriptive without options
- Overly technical without explanation
- Hard selling or pressure
- "Expert" or "guru" positioning

### Tone Guidelines
- **Openings**: Establish credibility naturally
- **Teaching**: Conversational and clear
- **Technical**: Precise but accessible
- **Conclusions**: Inspiring and actionable

## Credibility Integration

### Natural Authority Building
- "When we were building systems at Google..."
- "This workflow saved our enterprise clients hours..."
- "After a decade in UX at places like SAP..."
- Never force credentials, weave them naturally

### Experience References
- Mention past work only when directly relevant
- Focus on what viewers will achieve, not our achievements
- Use experience to validate techniques, not to boast

## Evolution Strategy

3. Are we maintaining 80/20 rule (core expertise vs personal)?
4. Time to introduce course content?
5. Which workflows resonated most?

### Content Evolution Path
1. **Phase 1**: Volume and experimentation
2. **Phase 2**: Focus on top performers
3. **Phase 3**: Systematic workflow libraries
4. **Phase 4**: Course content introduction
