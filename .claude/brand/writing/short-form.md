# Short-Form Writing Style Guide

## Quick Reference
- **Primary platforms:** Instagram/Threads, LinkedIn, Dribbble
- **Target audience:** UX designers, creative directors, developers, creatives (beginners to experts)
- **Voice attributes:** Relatable, cool, creative, intelligent, thought-provoking
- **Default perspective:** "We" (unless specified otherwise)
- **Default platform:** If unspecified, ask: "Which platform is this for - Instagram, LinkedIn, or Dribbble?"

## Core Voice & Tone

### Our Identity
We're creative technologists who make complex ideas accessible. We balance professional expertise with conversational warmth - knowledgeable but never condescending. Our content helps readers gain an edge in the rapidly evolving world of AI and design.

### Key Principles
- **Accessibility:** Write for high school reading level, then layer in expertise
- **Inspiration:** Help readers feel they're learning from special people who can elevate their work
- **Curiosity:** Ask questions, don't preach. We're thought-provokers, not know-it-alls
- **Inclusivity:** Everyone is welcome in our creative community

## Writing Guidelines

### Language & Accessibility
- Write at high school comprehension level
- Layer in expertise through examples, not jargon
- Define technical terms naturally in context
- Build from beginner to expert within posts

### Emphasis & Formatting
- **Bold** for key concepts
- *Italics* for subtle emphasis
- CAPS only for acronyms or extreme emphasis (rare)
- Numbers: Use numerals (7, not seven) for stats and counts
- Em dashes‚Äîfor dramatic pauses
- Ellipses... for trailing thoughts (sparingly)

### Engagement Techniques
- Ask genuine questions to spark discussion
- "What's your experience with..."
- "How do you approach..."
- "Have you noticed..."
- Share process, not just outcomes
- Invite collaboration and different perspectives
